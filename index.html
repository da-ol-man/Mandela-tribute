Nelson Mandela

“I have fought against white domination, and I have fought against black domination. I have cherished the ideal of a democratic and free society in which all persons live together in harmony and with equal opportunities. It is an ideal which I hope to live for and to achieve. But if needs be, it is an ideal for which I am prepared to die.”

from the dock speech by Nelson Mandela on 20 April 1964, during The Rivonia Trial

  • Nelson Mandela is regarded as an icon of freedom, justice and democracy and is the father of the South Africa the world knows today.
  • Born 18 July 1918 into the Madiba clan in the village of Mvezo, in the Eastern Cape.
  • He was a political activist and apartheid revolutionary.
  • He Joined the Afican National Congress (ANC) in 1943.
  • He helped co-found the ANC Youth-League in 1944.
  • When non-violent protests failed to produce results, he co-founded the militant ANC Umkhonto we Sizwe and lead a sabotage campaign against the South African apartheid government.
  • He was arrested and sentenced to life imprisonment in 1962 for conspiring to overthrow the government.
  • He served 27 years in prison and was realeased in 1990 by F.W. De Klerk.
  • He was elected as the first balck predident in the first free and fair elections of 1994.
  • He lead the efforts which established the new constitution of South Africa.
  • Nelson Mandela promoted peace and reconcilliaton and is a beacon of modern day leadership.
  • Nelson Mandela died 5 December 2013
